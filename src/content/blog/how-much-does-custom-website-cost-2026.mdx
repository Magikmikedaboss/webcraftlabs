## What Actually Affects Website Cost

Websites aren't priced by page count. They're priced by **complexity, responsibility, and outcomes**.

Here are the biggest factors that influence cost.

### 1. Custom Design vs Templates

Templates are cheaper because they're reused.

Custom design means the layout, structure, and user flow are built around your business, your audience, and your goals.

Templates help you get online.  
Custom design helps you stand out and convert.

---

### 2. Functionality & Integrations

Every added feature increases build time:

- booking systems  
- payment processing  
- membership portals  
- dashboards  
- CRM or API integrations  

The more your website needs to *do*, the more engineering it requires.

---

### 3. Messaging & Copywriting

Design doesn't sell by itself.

Clear messaging, strong positioning, and intentional copy often matter more than visuals. Many high-performing websites succeed because the message is right, not because the design is flashy.

---

### 4. SEO, Speed & Performance

In 2026, performance is not optional.

Speed, structure, accessibility, mobile optimization, and search visibility are foundational. These require technical planning, not drag-and-drop tools.

---

### 5. Who You Hire

Your choice of builder affects cost and outcome:

- **Freelancers**: lower cost, limited capacity  
- **Small studios**: strong balance of quality and accountability  
- **Agencies**: higher cost, more overhead  

You're not just paying for code — you're paying for experience, reliability, and decision-making.

---

## Why Cheap Websites Fail

Low-cost websites usually fail quietly.

They launch. They exist. They just don't perform.

Common issues include:

- slow load times  
- poor mobile experience  
- weak SEO structure  
- confusing navigation  
- generic layouts  
- no analytics or tracking  
- difficult updates  

A $500–$1,500 website often costs far more in lost leads and missed opportunities than it saves upfront.

## When a Custom Website Actually Makes Sense

A custom build matters when your website is more than a placeholder.

You should consider custom development if:

- your website drives revenue  
- you need integrations or automation  
- you want to rank in search results  
- you're building long-term brand credibility  
- your business is scaling  

If your site is part of how you win business, custom matters.
